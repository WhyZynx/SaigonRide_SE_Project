# SaigonRide – Distributed Vehicle Rental System

1. Background
Ho Chi Minh City is rapidly modernizing its public transit ecosystem to reduce traffic congestion and carbon emissions. As part of this initiative, SaigonRide is conceived as a distributed network of public bicycles and electric scooters. The system allows for high flexibility, enabling users to unlock a vehicle at one station (e.g., District 1) and return it to any other station within the network (e.g., District 2).

2. Problem Statement
Despite the potential, current transit solutions in the city face two major hurdles:

User Fragmentation: Existing systems fail to bridge the gap between Local Commuters, who prefer local e-wallets (MoMo, VNPay), and Foreign Tourists, who require international payment gateways (Apple Pay, PayPal) and mandatory passport verification.

Fleet Imbalance: The city struggles with manual redistribution, often leading to stations being either completely empty or overflowing. There is a lack of an automated incentive system to encourage users to assist in balancing the fleet.

3. Proposed Solution
Our team will develop a unified software platform to manage the SaigonRide ecosystem. The system will feature:

Dynamic Tiered Pricing: Automated fare calculation based on vehicle type (Standard Bike: 500 VND/min; E-Scooter: 1,500 VND/min).

Smart Incentives: A 15% discount engine triggered when users return vehicles to "Low-Inventory" stations (below 20% capacity) to promote self-balancing.

Dual-Channel Payment Architecture: A robust backend supporting both domestic and international payment methods, integrated with a secure verification module for tourists.

Administrative Analytics: Comprehensive reporting tools for fleet management and revenue tracking.
